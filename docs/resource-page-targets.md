# Resource Page Link Building: Target List & Playbook

A detailed guide to finding and acquiring links from resource pages in the haunted/paranormal niche.

---

## What Are Resource Pages?

Resource pages are curated lists of links on a specific topic. They exist to help visitors find useful content. Webmasters actively maintain them and are often happy to add quality resources.

**Why they're valuable:**
- Webmaster is already linking out (link-friendly mindset)
- Relevant to your niche (topical authority)
- Often on established sites (domain authority)
- Lower competition than news/PR outreach

These groups often maintain resource pages for their members and visitors.

| Type | What to Search | Link Page Location |
|------|----------------|-------------------|
| State paranormal societies | "[State] Paranormal Society" | Usually /links or /resources |
| Ghost hunting teams | "[City] Ghost Hunters" | Often in footer or About section |

**Search examples:**

## Finding Contact Information

### Method 1: Check the Website

Look for:
- Contact page
- About page (often has email)
- Footer (sometimes has webmaster email)
- "Submit a resource" or "Suggest a link" form

### Method 2: WHOIS Lookup

- Go to whois.domaintools.com
- Enter the domain
- Look for registrant email (often hidden now, but worth checking)

### Method 3: Hunter.io

- hunter.io (free tier available)
- Enter domain to find email patterns
- Shows verified emails associated with domain

### Method 4: LinkedIn

- Search for "[Site name] + webmaster/editor/owner"
- Connect or use InMail
- More personal but lower response rate

### Method 5: Social Media

- Check site's Twitter/Facebook for DM option
- Often faster response than email
- More casual tone acceptable

## Pro Tips

1. **Check if page is actively maintained** â€” Look for recent updates or "last updated" date. Dead pages won't add you.

2. **Study existing links** â€” What sites do they already link to? Match that quality level.

3. **Offer reciprocity** â€” "Happy to link back to your site" can increase success rate.

4. **Start with smaller sites** â€” DA 20-40 sites are more responsive than DA 60+ sites.

5. **Personalize genuinely** â€” Mention something specific about their site. "I noticed you cover [X]" shows you actually visited.

6. **Don't ask for specific anchor text** â€” Just ask for a link. Specifying anchor text looks spammy.

7. **Be patient** â€” Webmasters are busy. Response can take 1-2 weeks.

8. **Track domain authority gains** â€” Use Moz or Ahrefs free tools monthly to see if your DA is climbing.
